The Value of a Seller's Agent

Why the right listing agent can mean tens of thousands more in your pocket at closing.

Why "For Sale By Owner" Often Falls Short

It's tempting to think you can save money by selling your home without an agent. But research consistently shows that FSBO homes sell for significantly less than agent-listed properties — often enough to more than offset the cost of professional representation.

The reality is that selling a home involves complex pricing decisions, legal requirements, marketing strategies, and high-stakes negotiations. Most homeowners do this once or twice in a lifetime. A professional listing agent does it every day.

What a Seller's Agent Actually Does

Accurate Pricing

Overprice and your home sits on the market; underprice and you leave money on the table. Your agent uses real market data — not Zestimates — to find the sweet spot.

Professional Marketing

Professional photography, virtual tours, MLS syndication, social media campaigns, and targeted advertising that gets your listing in front of thousands of qualified buyers.

Buyer Network

Our agents have networks of active buyers, other agents with qualified clients, and investor contacts — giving your property exposure you simply can't replicate alone.

Legal Protection

Pennsylvania requires extensive disclosures and compliance with state and federal fair housing laws. Your agent ensures every requirement is met, protecting you from liability.

The Numbers Don't Lie

According to the National Association of Realtors, FSBO homes typically sell for about 23% less than agent-assisted homes. On a $350,000 home, that's potentially over $80,000 in lost value — far more than any commission fee.

Beyond the sale price, agents help you avoid common pitfalls that cost sellers money: accepting offers with weak financing that falls through, missing inspection deadlines, failing to negotiate repair credits properly, or pricing incorrectly from the start.
